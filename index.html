About Noor

Human interactions that nurture growth.

I am a Ph.D. student, early childhood educator, and emerging researcher whose work bridges human development, psychology, and culturally responsive early learning.

Inspired by the ways young children explore and make meaning in their worlds, my work centers moments of attunement, connection, and co-construction. I partner closely with educators to support the everyday practices that shape children’s learning, belonging, and well-being.

Evolving Positionality Statement

As an Iraqi-American educator and scholar-in-training, I understand research as something shaped by culture, place, memory, community, and lived experience.

Iraqi-American Educator and Scholar-in-Training

My work is shaped by the communities, classrooms, and cultural histories that have taught me to see education as relational, political, and deeply human. I bring my experiences as an early childhood educator, Iraqi-American learner, and emerging researcher into the questions I ask about children, teachers, families, and systems of care.

This section includes my evolving positionality statement, which reflects how I am thinking about identity, responsibility, and the kind of scholar I am becoming.
