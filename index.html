About Noor

Educator, researcher, and storyteller of early learning spaces.

Academic bio

My work examines the relationships, environments, and cultural practices that shape young children’s development. I am especially interested in how educators create emotionally responsive and culturally sustaining classrooms where children can explore, communicate, and belong.

Across research and practice, I partner closely with teachers to understand the everyday conditions that support children’s learning and educators’ well-being.

Positionality

My perspective is shaped by my experiences as an Iraqi-American educator, my work across early childhood classrooms, and my commitment to community-rooted, equity-oriented research.

Read more about my scholarly commitments

I approach early childhood research through a strengths-based lens that values family knowledge, cultural practices, teacher expertise, and children’s multimodal ways of making meaning.

Research

Research interests and developing projects

Human development

Teacher-child relationships

I study how attunement, emotional scaffolding, and everyday interactions support children’s social-emotional development.

Possible questions
  • How do teachers notice and respond to children’s emotional cues?
  • How do relational practices support children’s regulation and belonging?
Culturally responsive pedagogy

Classroom culture and belonging

I examine how classroom language, materials, routines, and documentation can affirm children’s identities and community knowledge.

Possible questions
  • How do classroom environments communicate whose stories matter?
  • How do educators build culturally responsive learning spaces with children?
Teacher well-being

Educator reflection and agency

I am interested in reflective spaces that help teachers process challenges, build trust, and transform classroom practice.

Possible questions
  • How do reflective teacher spaces support agency and collaboration?
  • What organizational conditions help teachers sustain responsive practice?

Teaching philosophy

I view classrooms as relational ecosystems. Teaching is not only the delivery of content; it is the design of conditions where children feel seen, capable, curious, and connected.
